The battle for America’s guns

There’s no denying the gun lobby’s influence in Washington. But depending on which side you advocate, it may not be such a bad thing.

    Another mass shooting, another futile attempt at passing meaningful gun control legislation. While gun violence continues to ravage our nation, one of the most influential groups in Washington - the gun lobby - continues its decades-long fight to block reasonable legislation. A look at how money is used to fight the war in Washington. (This will change depending on the slider)

    Consistently outspent Money spent by both gun rights groups and gun control groups to lobby Congress fell steadily towards the end of Bush's presidency and slowly increased during President Obama's first term. When Adam Lanza walked into Sandy Hook Elementary School and killed 28 children at the end of 2012, lobbying efforts on both sides spiked to recent highs.
